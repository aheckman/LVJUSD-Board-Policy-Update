# 4116 - Probationary/Permanent Status

**Type:** AR  
**Version:** Proposed  
**Status (from API):** DRAFT - KEEP CSBA  
**Original Adopted:** 10/01/1995  
**Last Revised:** Pending

## Policy Content

Eligibility for Permanent Status

A probationary employee who, in any one school year, has served for at least 75 percent of the number of days maintained by regular District schools shall be deemed to have served a complete school year.  (Education Code 44908)

The following shall not be included for purposes of computing the service required as a prerequisite to classification as a permanent employee:

	Service as an instructor in classes conducted at regional occupational centers or programs  (Education Code 44910)
	 
	Service under a provisional credential other than a one-year emergency credential (Education Code 44911)
	 
	Service only as a teacher of basic military drill in high school cadet companies  (Education Code 44912)
	 
	Employment in summer school  (Education Code 44913)

Permanency Achieved in Other Districts

A person who has achieved permanent status as a certificated employee in another school district may be employed by the Board of Education as a permanent certificated employee.  (Education Code 44929.28)

Interns

A person employed as a District or university intern shall be classified as a probationary employee.  Following completion of the internship, if reelected by the District to serve in a position requiring certification qualifications for the next succeeding school year, the employee shall continue to be classified as a probationary employee during that year.  (Education Code 44466, 44885.5)

An employee who has completed an internship and at least one complete school year in a position requiring certification qualifications within the District shall be granted permanent status when the employee is reelected for the next succeeding school year to a position requiring certification qualifications.  (Education Code 44466, 44885.5)

# 6159.3 - Appointment Of Surrogate Parent For Special Education Students

**Type:** BP  
**Version:** Current  
**Status (from API):** ADOPTED  
**Original Adopted:** 09/01/1992  
**Last Revised:** 03/01/2005

---

## Policy Content

CSBA NOTE: The following mandated policy and accompanying administrative regulation reflect the federal Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) (20 USC 1400-1482), as reauthorized in 2004 pursuant to P.L. 108-446, effective July 1, 2005. Note that in cases where state law provides greater protections, state law supersedes federal law.Pursuant to 20 USC 1415(b)(2), as amended, districts and/or Special Education Local Plan Areas (SELPAs) are mandated to adopt procedures to protect the rights of students whenever (1) the parents of the student are not known, (2) the district cannot, after reasonable efforts, locate the parents, or (3) the student is a ward of the state. When the student is a ward of the court, the district's procedures must also include the assignment of an individual to act as a surrogate for the parents. See the accompanying administrative regulation for language implementing this mandate.This policy and the following regulation should be revised for consistency with the policy and regulations of the SELPA in which the district participates.
In order to protect the rights of students with disabilities, the Superintendent or designee shall appoint a surrogate parent to represent a student with disabilities as necessary in accordance with law.Â  He/she shall develop procedures for recruiting, training and assigning adults to serve as surrogate parents.
